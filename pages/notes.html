n. A personal journal in which quotable passages, literary excerpts, and comments are written.

The state-of-the-art in annotating, glossing, transcluding, collaborating on, remixing, drawing on, and composing text is all unacceptable. The current approaches are closed-source, overly linear, insufficiently visual, subject to data lock-in, and/or encourage premature labeling of unformed ideas. This page is for experiments in changing all that.

Ideas, case studies, avenues

  • I've used Evernote for years because it was the least bad option available, and now I have major data lock-in. Liberating my data is a work-in-progress.
  • Twitter is a nice place to ideate and receive feedback, but the archiving is not great. In alignment with #indieweb principles, I intend to POSSE or PESOS my twitter feed. I also need to quit using favorites as a way of saving tweets I'd like to archive.
  • Amber Case has a public wiki. Anyone she follows on twitter can log in to edit it.
  • AnnotateJS intrigues me but I haven't used it.
